****** Aloo Paratha recipe| Potato Stuffed Paratha ******

aloo paratha recipe | aalu ka paratha | aloo ka paratha | potato paratha with detailed photo and video recipe. a classical north indian stuffed bread recipe made with w heat flour and spiced potato mash for stuffing. it is one of the popular paratha recipes which is not only easy to make but also one of the tasty stuffed bread from the indian cuisine. it is generally served for lunch and dinner with a choice of raita and pickle but can also be served for morning breakfast and brunch.

Ingredients for dough:

  1. 2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)
  2. 1/2 teaspoon salt
  3. 1 tablespoon oil
  4. Water (as needed)

For the potato filling:

  1. 3 to 4 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
  2. 1 small onion, finely chopped (optional)
  3. 2 to 3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  4. 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  5. 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  6. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  7. 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice preference)
  8. 1 tablespoon chopped cilantro (coriander leaves)
  9. Salt to taste
  10. Oil or ghee (clarified butter) for cooking the parathas

Instructions To prepare aalo paratha

  1. Making the dough:

    • In a large mixing bowl, add the whole wheat flour and salt. Mix well.
    • Slowly add water and knead the dough until it becomes smooth and elastic. Add a little oil and knead again to coat the dough with oil. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for about 15-20 minutes.

  3. Preparing the potato filling:

    • Boil the potatoes until they are fully cooked. You can either boil them in a pressure cooker or on the stovetop. Once boiled, peel the skin and mash them well using a potato masher or fork. Ensure there are no lumps.
    • In a mixing bowl, add the mashed potatoes, finely chopped onions (if using), green chilies, cumin seeds, garam masala, turmeric powder, red chili powder, chopped cilantro, and salt. Mix everything thoroughly until the spices are well incorporated with the potatoes.

  4. Assembling and rolling the parathas:

    • Divide the dough into equal-sized balls (approximately the size of a golf ball) and roll them into smooth balls.
    • Take one dough ball and flatten it slightly. Dust it with some dry wheat flour and roll it out into a small disc, about 4-5 inches in diameter.
    • Place a portion of the potato filling (about 2-3 tablespoons) in the center of the disc.
    • Gather the edges of the disc towards the center, covering the filling entirely, and pinch the dough to seal it properly.
    • Flatten this stuffed ball with your palms and dust it with some dry flour.
    • Using a rolling pin, gently roll out the stuffed ball into a circular paratha. Be careful not to press too hard as it may break, and the filling might come out.

  5. Cooking the Aloo Parathas:

    • Heat a tawa or flat skillet over medium heat. Once it's hot, place the rolled paratha on it.
    • Cook one side until you see small bubbles forming on the surface. Then, flip the paratha and cook the other side.
    • Apply some oil or ghee on the cooked side, spreading it evenly. Flip the paratha again and apply oil on the other side as well.
    • Press the edges with a spatula to ensure even cooking and cook until both sides are golden brown and crispy.

  6. Serving Aloo Paratha:

    • Once the Aloo Paratha is cooked, remove it from the tawa and place it on a plate.
    • Serve the hot and delicious Aloo Paratha with plain yogurt, pickle, chutney, or even a dollop of butter on top.
Enjoy your homemade Aloo Parathas! They are best enjoyed fresh and hot. You can customize the spices according to your taste preferences.
